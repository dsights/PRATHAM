1. System Architecture Overview

The system creates a real-time digital replica of the entire supply chain, from raw materials to final delivery, using AI agents to negotiate and optimize routes.

Structural Pillars:

  • Data Fabric: Unified layer connecting ERP, CRM, and IoT data.
  • Digital Twin Service: State machine tracking inventory, lead times, and transit status.
  • Agentic Layer: Specialized AI agents (Procurement Agent, Logistics Agent, Demand Agent).
  • Optimization Engine: Constraint solvers combined with Reinforcement Learning.

2. Problem Statement

Global supply chains are fragile and reactive.

  • Information Lag: Delays in reporting port congestion lead to stockouts.
  • Manual Planning: Humans cannot optimize for 100,000+ SKUs across 50 countries simultaneously.
  • Bullwhip Effect: Small changes in consumer demand result in massive over-ordering due to lack of visibility.

3. Engineering Approach to Solve the Problem

  • Event-Driven Architecture: Every delay is an event that triggers an "Auto-Correction" workflow.
  • Multi-Agent Systems (MAS): Instead of one big algorithm, use small agents that "negotiate" with each other (e.g., The Trucking Agent asks the Warehouse Agent for a slot).
  • Graph Data Fabric: Model the supply chain as a graph to easily perform "Path Finding" and "Impact Analysis" when a node (factory) fails.

4. The AI Advantage Used

  • Reinforcement Learning (RL): The system learns optimal "Re-order Points" by simulating millions of supply chain scenarios in a sandbox.
  • NLP for Document Processing: AI agents read "Bills of Lading" and "Invoices" (PDFs) to automatically update the Digital Twin.
  • Demand Forecasting: Transformer models analyze social trends, weather, and historical data to predict surges with 95% accuracy.
